## Machine Learning:

### Learning Objectives:
1. Understand the basic concepts and terminology of machine learning.
2. Identify different types of machine learning approaches and algorithms.
3. Understand the process of training, validating, and testing a machine learning model.
4. Interpret and evaluate the performance of a machine learning model.
5. Recognize the ethical implications and potential biases in machine learning.

### Key Knowledge Requirements:
1. Definition of machine learning and related terms (e.g., features, labels, training data).
2. Types of ML (Supervised, Unsupervised, Semi-supervised, Reinforcement Learning).
3. Popular algorithms (e.g., Linear Regression, Decision Trees, Neural Networks).
4. Evaluation metrics (e.g., Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1 Score).
5. Data preprocessing techniques (e.g., normalization, handling missing data).
6. Understanding of overfitting, underfitting, and model selection.
7. Ethical considerations (e.g., data privacy, bias in training data).

## Cybersecurity:

### Learning Objectives:
1. Understand the basic principles and concepts of cybersecurity.
2. Recognize common types of cyber threats and attacks.
3. Identify basic cybersecurity best practices and defensive measures.
4. Understand the role and importance of various cybersecurity technologies and tools.
5. Recognize the legal and ethical responsibilities related to cybersecurity.

### Key Knowledge Requirements:
1. Definition of cybersecurity and related terms (e.g., malware, phishing, firewall).
2. Types of cyber threats (e.g., Virus, Trojan, Ransomware, DDoS attack).
3. Defensive measures (e.g., strong passwords, two-factor authentication, encryption).
4. Network security basics (e.g., Firewalls, VPNs, Intrusion Detection Systems).
5. Legal regulations related to cybersecurity (e.g., GDPR, CCPA).
6. Ethical hacking and the role of a white-hat hacker.
7. Incident response and recovery strategies.
